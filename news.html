2014–2018

2 CIF Titles • State Coach of the Year
June 2018

Press Box Dedicated to Gordy Thompson

Nearly 50 friends, family, and colleagues gathered at Granite Hills Field for a celebration of life for legendary coach Gordy Thompson, who passed away December 31, 2017 at age 76. The press box was dedicated in his memory, with a sign unveiled by his family. Former players Brian and Marcus Giles attended. Thompson coached at Granite Hills for 20 years, compiling a 379-213 record with 8 league titles and 3 CIF championships. Read more →

June 2018

Former Eagle Jordan Verdon Signs with Detroit Tigers

After earning First Team All-Mountain West honors at San Diego State, former Granite Hills standout Jordan Verdon was selected in the 23rd round of the 2018 MLB Draft by the Detroit Tigers. Verdon had originally been drafted in the 21st round by the Mets in 2015 out of Granite Hills. Read more →

June 2017

Sean Ross Drafted by Toronto Blue Jays

Granite Hills outfielder Sean Ross was selected in the 40th round of the 2017 MLB Draft by the Toronto Blue Jays. Ross hit .359 as a senior with 8 home runs, 19 RBI, 15 doubles, and 21 runs scored. He went on to play at San Diego State.

June 2016

Eagles Win CIF Division I Championship over Vista

Granite Hills captured its second CIF title in three years, defeating Vista for the Division I crown. Senior pitcher Dillan Shrum threw five strong innings for the win, calling it his second championship in four years. Trevor Omahen and Josh Verdon anchored the infield for a squad that had watched the 2014 title from the bench as freshmen. Read more →

June 2015

Jordan Verdon Drafted by New York Mets

Granite Hills third baseman Jordan Verdon was selected in the 21st round (pick 719) of the 2015 MLB Draft by the New York Mets, continuing the school's tradition of producing professional ballplayers. Read more →

June 2014

Eagles Capture CIF Open Division Title; Davis Named State Coach of the Year

Granite Hills won the elite CIF San Diego Section Open Division championship, the highest classification in the section. Head coach James Davis was named California State Coach of the Year by Cal-Hi Sports for leading the Eagles to the prestigious title.

2005–2007

2007 CIF Champions • Davis Era Begins
June 2007

Eagles Win CIF Division I Championship, 3-2 over Torrey Pines

Granite Hills captured the CIF San Diego Section Division I title with a thrilling 3-2 victory over Torrey Pines, marking Coach James Davis's first section championship. The win established Davis as a rising coaching star in San Diego County and kicked off what would become one of the most dominant eras in program history. Read more →

June 2005

Eagles Reach CIF Division I Finals

In only his fourth season leading the program, Coach James Davis guided Granite Hills to the CIF San Diego Section Division I championship game. Though the Eagles fell short in the finals, the appearance signaled that Granite Hills had re-established itself among the elite programs in the section.

2001

James Davis Takes Over as Head Coach

Former San Diego State pitcher James Davis was hired as head coach, beginning what would become one of the most successful tenures in San Diego Section history. Davis, who pitched collegiately under legendary coach Jim Dietz and later played in an independent league, brought a new energy to the storied program.

1994–1998

The Thompson Dynasty • No. 1 in the Nation
1998

Eagles Ranked No. 1 in the Nation

Granite Hills captured its third CIF championship under Coach Gordy Thompson and was ranked the No. 1 high school baseball team in the entire country by USA Today. The 1998 squad featured several future professional players and capped Thompson's remarkable dynasty.

1996

Marcus Giles: Following in His Brother's Footsteps

Marcus Giles graduated from Granite Hills and was drafted by the Atlanta Braves. The younger brother of Brian Giles, Marcus would go on to become a standout second baseman in the majors and later reunite with Brian on the 2007 San Diego Padres.

1994

Eagles Win 1994 CIF Championship Behind Future Pros

Coach Gordy Thompson led Granite Hills to the CIF San Diego Section championship. The squad featured John Barnes, who would go on to win the PCL batting title in 2000 (.365 avg) with the Minnesota Twins organization. The '94 title cemented Thompson's program as a perennial powerhouse.

1990

Shane Spencer Drafted by New York Yankees

Granite Hills outfielder Shane Spencer was selected in the 28th round of the 1990 MLB Draft by the New York Yankees. Spencer would later become famous for his incredible September 1998 call-up, hitting 10 home runs in 67 at-bats as the Yankees won 114 games and the World Series.

1989

Brian Giles Drafted by Cleveland Indians

Granite Hills standout Brian Giles was selected in the 17th round of the 1989 MLB Draft by the Cleveland Indians. Named San Diego County Male Athlete of the Year, Giles went on to a stellar 15-year MLB career as a two-time All-Star, hitting .293 with 287 home runs and 1,030 RBI. He was later inducted into the Granite Hills Hall of Fame in 2008.

Eagles Legacy

8 CIF Titles • 9 MLB Players • 41 Draft Picks
1977–Present

8 CIF San Diego Section Championships

Granite Hills has won 8 CIF San Diego Section baseball championships: 1977, 1994, 1998, 2007, 2014, 2016, 2021, and 2025. Only two coaches have led the Eagles to titles: Gordy Thompson (1977, 1994, 1998) and James Davis (2007, 2014, 2016, 2021, 2025).

Program History

9 Major Leaguers and 41 Draft Picks

The Eagles have produced 9 players who reached the Major Leagues, including two-time All-Star Brian Giles and World Series champion Shane Spencer. A total of 29 players have been drafted directly from the school, and 41 total draft picks have come through the program across its history.

Two Coaching Legends

Thompson & Davis: A Combined 900+ Wins

Gordy Thompson (379-213, 3 CIF titles, 8 league titles) and James Davis (500+ wins, 5 CIF titles, State Coach of the Year) have built Granite Hills into one of the most respected high school baseball programs in California. Thompson's press box dedication in 2018 and Davis's 500th win in 2024 mark the enduring impact of both coaches.

1977

Where It All Started: 1977 CIF Championship

Coach Gordy Thompson led Granite Hills to its first-ever CIF San Diego Section baseball championship in 1977, launching a tradition of excellence that has now spanned nearly five decades and produced some of the finest players in San Diego County history.
